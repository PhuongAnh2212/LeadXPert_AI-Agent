Quiet Hours helps you pause interruptions during times when you do not want routine notifications to appear. It is designed for overnight rest, deep work, travel, or any recurring window where you want the app to hold back non-urgent updates. Critical notifications, such as account security alerts, billing failures, outage notices, or required action messages, can still appear during Quiet Hours because they may need immediate attention.

To set up Quiet Hours, open Smart Notification Center, choose Notification Settings, and select Quiet Hours. Before you choose a schedule, the app explains how permissions affect delivery. If push notifications are disabled at the device level, the app may not be able to show alerts immediately after Quiet Hours ends. On iOS and Android, system notification settings can also affect whether Critical, Normal, and Low notifications appear as expected.

Choose a start time, an end time, and the days when the schedule should run. The suggested schedule is 10:00PM to 7:00AM local time, but you can adjust the window to match your routine. You can select weekdays, weekends, or custom days. When Quiet Hours is active, Normal and Low notifications are held until the quiet window ends. When the window ends, held notifications become available in the unified inbox and may also appear in the Weekly Digest if they are digest-eligible.

You can pause Quiet Hours temporarily for 1 hour, 4 hours, or until tomorrow. This is useful if you are on call, traveling across time zones, or expecting time-sensitive updates that are not classified as Critical. After the pause expires, your saved Quiet Hours schedule resumes automatically.
